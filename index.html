Clarifying Terminology

Session
Single visit, 1 or more pages, up to 30 minutes (Google Analytics default)
Landing Page
The page the user enters your site
Channel
Combines source + medium. Organic, Paid, Social, Email
Segment
A subset of users
Conversion
Completed Activity

Bounce vs. Exit

Bounce
User session only contains a single page view
Exit
For all pageviews, the percentage that were the last in the session.
Page Bounce Rate Exit Rate
Home > Product > Cart > Exit Home 0% 50%
Product > Exit Product 33% 33%
Product > Home > Exit Cart 0% 100%

The metrics you care about should be a reflection of your strategy.

Types of Metrics

Vanity — BEWARE

Look good on paper, but not actionable

Types of Metrics

Engagement

  • Extremely important for early stage startups
  • User validation and product/market fit
  • Learn more about your users, your demographic, your technology habits
  • Better engagement = more set up for growth later

Types of Metrics

Engagement

  • Returning Users
  • Number of Downloads
  • Mailing Signups
  • Session Length & Interval
  • Acquisition & Referral

Types of Metrics

Growth

  • HOT Topic, but not always the right thing to watch
  • Focus on the most important metric for right now, while watching for glaring red flags

Types of Metrics

Growth

  • Conversion Rate
  • Revenue - MRR/ARR
  • Churn
  • Lifetime Customer Value
  • Cost to Acquire Customer

Types of Metrics

Benefit

Another, very UX focused, way of thinking
  • Rather than optimize for inward analytics, optimize for what your customer is measuring
  • "Measure the lives you save, not the life preservers you sell" - Andrew Chen

Types of Metrics

Operational vs Investigative

  • Operational = Task or Goal at hand
  • Investigative = Curiosity, trends

Pro Tip

Best to measure the rate of change, not the totals.

Totals should always go up, but the rate of change will vary.

LEAN into it

More data != Better

What's your current goal? Measure that, and forget the rest. When the goal changes, your metrics change.

Example Product Roadmap

To-Do App

  • 1) Build Initial MVP

    Users can add and remove items from a personal to-do list

  • 2) Initial Tracking

    How many new users, how many recurring, how many items they add and remove in a given period

  • 3) Grow

    Add new features and start promoting

    Start tracking things like churn, DAU/MAU, etc.

  • 4) Iterate & Evolve

    Add new features, test, track. Was it good or bad? Learn from it. Repeat.

    Track where new users are coming from. Annotating is helpful.

Things To Avoid

  • Success Theatre with Vanity Metrics
  • Mimicking other strategy when your goals aren't the same
  • Spending time on numbers and reports you'll never use

"Terrible" Metrics Are NORMAL

"Terrible" Metrics Are NORMAL

It's actually common for

  • Signup rates to be below 1%
  • Over 95% of your users to be inactive today
  • Lonely hearts club for your users: Over half won't know anyone else

All of this takes time, but what's Better?

building 10 features where you don’t know what worked and what didn’t, or building less features but have a clear sense for what and why something worked?
