Why Are We Here?

Metrics ground our assumptions in facts, and allow us to verify and form new ones.

Measure our progress towards goals and new new goals rooted in what's real.

Validate the vision.

Helps to remove some bias and ego from decision making process.

Informed decisions, identify trends and patterns, problem areas, next steps.

Funding! Many VC's want to know real numbers to better understand your customer engagement including growth loops, acquisition, churn, and revenue.

Is it better to build 10 features where you don’t know what worked and what didn’t, or is it better to build LESS features but have a clear sense for what and why something worked?

Terminology

Session
Single visit, 1 or more pages, up to 30 minutes (Google Analytics default)
Landing Page
The page the user enters your site
Channel
Combines source + medium. Organic, Paid, Social, Email
Bounce
User session only contains a single page view
Exit
For all pageviews, the percentage that were the last in the session.
Segment
A subset of users
Conversion
Completed Activity

Types of Metrics

Engagement

Better engagement = more set up for growth later

Learn more about your users, your demographic, your technology habits

User validation and product/market fit

Growth

Conversion rate, Email Sign Ups, Returning Users, DAU/MAU

Focus on the most important metric for right now, while watching for glaring red flags

Benefit

Rather than optimize for inward analytics, optimize for what your customer is measuring. "Measure the lives you save, not the life preservers you sell" - Andrew Chen

Operational vs Investigative

LEAN into it

The metrics you care about should be a reflection of your strategy.

More data != Better

No analytics: Act on gut, often fall to trends rather than usefulness, overly vocal early users and knee jerk ideas and reactions can lead you astray.

Too many analytics: They recognize the importance of analytics, often to a fault. Constantly chasing their tail without a strategy. What's your current goal? Measure that, and forget the rest. When the goal changes, your metrics change.

Example product roadmap I’ll close out this post with an example roadmap for a product that roughly had the same featureset as Imageshack.us. You may end up making these product decision out-of-order of what I have them, but the philosophy remains the same: Build analytics only when you need them, and align them to the key efforts behind your product development process. Here’s an example sequencing of product development: 1) Core product and features Obviously, you want people to be able to upload a photo and give a URL for it These photos then can be displayed on other sites 1a) Analytics for core product First add Google analytics since it’s free :-) Build out a cohort matrix that tells you how many users join each week, how many come back, and what they do during their visits Where are these images being uploaded to? A breakdown of image displays by the domain they were displayed on would be nice 2) Working on growing traffic Once you have the core product done, perhaps you want to make some whizbang features to grow traffic You might make a photo slideshow, or some awesome photo effects, etc. Also, you might want to make one-click integration with Typepad/MySpace/etc via something like Gigya 2a) Analytics for traffic growth features One key issue to measure is how much additional traffic is gained by these different methods. You may want to start annotating visits that come to your site based on what kind of widget or photo created it. Do sparkly photo galleries generate the most visits? Why? Make a report Second feature might be an A/B testing framework. If you divide two groups, A and B, and show them different featuresets to see how the traffic grows for each

Do's and Don'ts

Success Theatre

Mimick others strategy if the goals aren't the same

Long term and short term goals (balance)

Terrible Metrics Are NORMAL

It's actually common for:

Signup rates to be below 1%.

Over 95% of your users to be inactive today.

Over half of users don't know anyone else that uses the service.
